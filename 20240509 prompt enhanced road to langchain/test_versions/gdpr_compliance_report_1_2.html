As an IT company, mitigating GDPR risks is crucial to avoid potential fines and reputational damage. Here's a simplified guide to help you get started:

**What are the penalties for non-compliance with GDPR?**

The maximum fine for non-compliance with GDPR can be up to 4% of your global annual turnover or €20 million (whichever is greater). This means that if your company has an annual revenue of, say, €100 million, the maximum fine could be €4 million.

**What should you do to mitigate GDPR risks?**

1. **Conduct a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)**: Identify potential data processing activities and assess their impact on individuals' privacy.
2. **Map your data flows**: Track where personal data is stored, processed, and shared within your organization.
3. **Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures**: Ensure you have robust security measures in place to protect personal data (e.g., encryption, access controls).
4. **Provide transparency and consent**: Clearly communicate how you process personal data and obtain explicit consent from individuals where necessary.
5. **Designate a Data Protection Officer (DPO)**: Appoint someone responsible for ensuring GDPR compliance within your organization.

**Examples of GDPR non-compliance:**

1. **Lack of consent**: A company collects personal data without obtaining explicit consent from individuals, leading to a fine of €10 million.
2. **Data breach**: A company fails to notify the relevant authorities and affected individuals about a data breach, resulting in a fine of €5 million.
3. **Insufficient security measures**: A company stores sensitive personal data in an unencrypted format, leading to a fine of €2 million.

**Additional tips:**

1. **Stay up-to-date with GDPR guidelines**: Regularly review the official GDPR documentation and guidance from the European Data Protection Board (EDPB).
2. **Train your staff**: Educate employees on GDPR requirements and ensure they understand their roles in maintaining compliance.
3. **Conduct regular audits**: Periodically assess your organization's GDPR compliance to identify areas for improvement.

By following these steps, you'll be well on your way to mitigating GDPR risks and avoiding potential fines. Remember, GDPR is a continuous process that requires ongoing effort and attention.
