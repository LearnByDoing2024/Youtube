**Mitigating GDPR Risk**

To mitigate GDPR risk, I recommend:

• **Conduct regular data audits**: Identify and map all personal data processed by your company.
• **Implement robust security measures**: Use encryption, access controls, and secure storage for sensitive data.
• **Train employees**: Educate staff on GDPR requirements and best practices for handling personal data.
• **Develop a breach response plan**: Establish procedures for reporting and responding to data breaches.

**Penalties for Non-Compliance with GDPR**

If your company fails to comply with GDPR regulations, you may face:

• **Fines**: Up to €20 million or 4% of your global annual turnover (whichever is greater).
• **Reprimands**: Instead of fines, a reprimand may be issued for minor infringements.
• **Criminal penalties**: In some cases, criminal charges may be brought against individuals responsible for non-compliance.

**Examples**

* A company fails to notify the data subject about a personal data breach, resulting in a fine of €10 million.
* An employee accidentally sends sensitive customer data via email, and the company is reprimanded instead of fined due to the minor nature of the infringement.

Remember, GDPR compliance is crucial for protecting your customers' personal data. By implementing robust security measures and training employees, you can minimize the risk of non-compliance and avoid severe penalties!
