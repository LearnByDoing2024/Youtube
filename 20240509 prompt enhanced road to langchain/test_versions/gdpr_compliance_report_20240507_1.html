Let's break it down in a simple way!

**Mitigating GDPR Risk**

To mitigate GDPR risk, I recommend:

• **Conducting regular data audits**: Identify and map your company's personal data processing activities to ensure compliance.
• **Implementing robust security measures**: Use encryption, secure storage, and access controls to protect sensitive data.
• **Training employees**: Educate staff on GDPR requirements, data handling procedures, and the importance of data protection.
• **Establishing incident response plans**: Develop a plan for responding to data breaches or other incidents.

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**

If your company fails to comply with GDPR regulations, you may face:

• **Fines**: Up to 4% of your global annual turnover (€20 million maximum).
• **Reprimands**: Instead of fines, a reprimand may be issued for minor infringements or if the fine would cause disproportionate burden.
• **Criminal penalties**: In some cases, criminal charges and imprisonment may apply.

**Examples**

* A company fails to notify data subjects about a personal data breach within 72 hours. They could face a fine of up to €10 million (1% of their global annual turnover).
* An organization uses inadequate security measures, resulting in the theft of sensitive customer data. They might receive a reprimand for minor infringement.
* A company deliberately ignores GDPR requirements and fails to protect personal data. In this case, criminal charges and imprisonment could be imposed.

Remember, it's essential to prioritize data protection and take proactive steps to ensure compliance with GDPR regulations!
